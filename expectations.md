Format

    10–15 minutes of presentation, followed by 15–20 minutes of questions.
    You may present alone or in a group of two. If you present as a group, each of you must deliver one half of the talk.
    Send us your slide deck and your code by the evening before your presentation at the latest. This is a hard requirement: failure to submit on time will result in a failing grade.

What we expect you to have built

    A hybrid, GPU-accelerated and MPI-parallelized code, if you implement the Lattice Boltzmann method with Kokkos; or
    A GPU-accelerated version (without MPI),
    if you use one of the programming models not covered directly in the lecture (e.g. HIP/ROCm, Fortran, JAX, PyTorch)
    or implement a numerical method other than Lattice Boltzmann.

Your talk should

    explain what your code does;
    convince us that it actually does what you claim;
    demonstrate the performance and scaling of the code;
    optionally, entertain.
